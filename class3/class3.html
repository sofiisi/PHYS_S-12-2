Circuit

I had to be honest and said that I built the real circuit first. I played around Tinkercad, and I didn't like it. I feel it was to slow for me. However, after I finished my circuit, I copied it to Tinkercad because I find it useful to show other people what I'm doing more clearly. I used a DC motor (a CC one in the Tinkercad), a 1K resistor, and a led. Well, and all the other basics materials.
What I learned so far is to recognize the cathode and the anode because I committed that mistake at least ten times. I understand how electricity ”moves” around the breadboard. How to use a potentiometer. How to not connect the wires and how to strip them. Because, despite my experience with electronics and Arduino, I had always used male to male jumper wires, so I had never striped one.
